Robert Luis Stevenson

Life

- 1850 He was born in Edinburgh, Scotland.
There he studied law, but since he had little or no taste for the legal profession and was suffering from tubercolosis.
- 1875 He emerged from law school in 1875 but did not practice, as, by this point, he felt that his calling was to be a writer.
- 1880 He married Fanny Osbourne, she was 36- years old,and she had two children with another man.
- 1886 This was the year of the publication of "Strange case of Dr.Jekyll and Mr. Hyde"
- 1894 He died

The strange case of Dr.Jekyll and Mr. Hyde

Is a story set in London. PRINCIPAL THEMES: The duality in the human nature: The theme of the "dualism" spread between the end of the 19th century and the beginning of the 20th century.
There was some writers that started talking about this on their works. They were able to penetrate the essence of Man's nature and to express a new philosophy which investigated human life and people's minds. Denunciation of the Victorian Society: The two sides of Dr.Jekylls's personality are using to describe the dualistic nature of the Victorian society. Where you must be respectable on the outside while the bad side is kept under control. Development of the science: We see it in the potion that was created to trasform Dr. Jekyll in Mr.Hyde.


The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde talks about Dr. Jekyll who studying the human psyche, creates a potion that can separate the two natures of the human soul, the good and the evil. His personality is divided into two parts and can't revert. DR JEKYLL: Is a respectable man. He's known in the city like a good man and person. That's the good side of himself. MR HYDE: He is shorter and younger than Jekyll because the good side is always more developed than the bad one. When the people met Mr.Hyde they are deeply impressed by his look(very ugly). He is violent and commits terrible crimes like the dead of the little girl. Jekyll was able to controlled the transformations with the serum, but one night, he became Hyde involuntarily in his sleep. Jekyll tried to cease becoming Hyde, but, another night, he drank the serum and Hyde, furious, killed Carew. Jekyll tried again to stop the transformations, but in January he turned into the man involuntarily while awake. Hunted by the police as a murderer, Hyde needed help to avoid capture, so he wrote to Lanyon, asking him to bring chemicals from his laboratory. Hyde drank the serum, transforming into Jekyll in front of Lanyon, who was so shock he died. Meanwhile, Jekyll's involuntarily transformations increased: fearing he would become Hyde permanently, Dr Jekyll decided to kill himself.
