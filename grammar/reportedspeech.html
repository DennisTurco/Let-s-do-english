REPORTED SPEECH
Il discorso indiretto si usa per riferire quello che qualcuno ha detto.
Il cambiamento da diretto ad indiretto avviene nei:
- verbi
- pronomi personali
- pronomi possessivi
- aggettivi possessivi
- aggettivi dimostrativi
- espressioni di luogo e tempo
DIRECT SPEECH REPORTED SPEECH
PRESENT SIMPLE
"He always GREETS me" 		PAST SIMPLE
I said he always GREETED me.
PRESENT CONTINUOUS
"My sister IS LISTENING to some music" 		PAST CONTINUOUS
She said her sister WAS LISTENING to some music.
PRESENT PERFECT
"I'VE already MET Eddie" 		PAST PERFECT
Mrs Ryan SAID he HAD already MET Eddie.
SIMPLE PAST
"Dad LEFT two days ago" 		PAST PERFECT
I said dad HAD LEFT two days ago.
PAST CONTINUOUS
"We WERE PLAYING cards at 9 p.m. last night 		PAST CONTINUOUS
They said they WERE PLAYING cards at 9 p.m. the previous night.
PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS
"I remember I HAD BEEN LOOKING at you for too many time" 		PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS
She said she HAD BEEN LOOKING at him for too many time
PAST PERFECT
"I already told you we HAD MET last week" 		PAST PERFECT
I said we HAD already MET the previous week.
WILL FUTURE
"I'LL HELP you do your homework" 		WOULD
Peppy said she WOULD HELP me do my homework.
TO BE GOING TO (future)
"Betty'S GOING TO FAINT" 		TO BE GOING TO (past)
I said Betty WAS GOING TO FAINT.
SIMPLE CONDITIONAL
"We WOULD LIKE to live our house" 		SIMPLE CONDITIONAL
They said they WOULD LIKE to live their house.
CAN
"We CAN SWIM very well" 		COULD
The two girls said they COULD SWIM very well.
MAY (eventualità)
"She MAY VISIT her grandparents" 		WAS/WERE LIKELY TO
He said she WAS LIKELY TO VISIT her grandparents.
MAY (permesso)
"MAY I TAKE the test with me?" 		MIGHT
She asked me whether if she MIGHT TAKE the test with her.
MUST (deduction)
"I MUST HAVE BROKEN my leg" 		MUST
He said he MUST HAVE BROKEN his leg.
MUST (obligation)
"You MUST RESPECT the rules" 		HAD TO
I told them they HAD TO RESPECT the rules.
HAVE TO
"We HAVE TO DO our homework" 		HAD TO
They said they HAD TO DO their homework.
MIGHT/SHOULD/COULD
"You SHOULD BUY this beautiful dress" 		MIGHT/SHOULD/COULD
I told her she SHOULD BUY that beautiful dress.
IMPERATIVE
"CHECK your wallet!" 		INFINITIVE
Mum ordered me TO CHECK my wallet.

INTRODUZIONE REPORTED SPEECH
-TELL+COMPLEMENTO OGGETTO: tell viene utilizzato quando nel discorso indiretto c'è una persona a cui rivolgersi.
Sam TOLD US he was going to get married.

-SAY: say viene utilizzato quando nel discorso indiretto non c'è una persona a cui rivolgersi. They SAID they were going to the cinema

-ASKED: asked viene utilizzato se il discorso indiretto riporta una domanda D.S: "Could I have two more slices of bread?"
R.S: She ASKED me whether she could have two more slices of bread.

-ORDERED: ordered viene utilizzato con l'imperativo quando ciò che viene riportato è un ordine D.S: "Do your homework"
R.S: Mum ORDERED me to do my homework.
