Suicide in the Trenches

Siegfried Sassoon was a war poet who used his poems to talk about the anger that people felt when they realized the lie of war retoric.


Summary

The poem is about a young soldier who go to war.
When he was young he felt joyful and he was very optimistic about life, he slept safe and sound at night and he wasn't scared.
After becoming a soldier the boy committed suicide: he felt miserable and desperate because there were no intertainment and he couldn't face the harshness of trenches.
No one spoke of him again because committing suicide meant dishonor. It meant you didn't have the courage to face death.

Analysis

The poem begins in the soldier's house, when he was young and happy and it's spring time. It is symbolic because spring is a joyful season as the boy was at the time.
When he went to war the season changes, now it's winter: the soldier is unhappy, he feels miserable and he commits suicide.
This poem is addressed to those who stayed at home and watched the war instead of feeling it. Sassoon says that they should pray that they will never know what war is.
The form and the rhyme reminds to a poem for children: it's easy and seams a lullaby and you're not prepared to face the harshness and cruelty that the poem represents.
The poem is both a war and anti-war poem: it's pro war because it actually is about the experience of war. But it's also anti-war because it denounces the atrocity behind it.
